About

I am a joint Ph.D. candidate in Materials Engineering (University of Siegen) and Chemistry (Hasselt University). My research lies at the interface of light–matter interaction and electrochemical energy storage. I design tungsten-oxide and diamond-based photoelectrodes, build WO₃/TiO₂ and WO₃/BDD heterojunctions, and study their in/operando behaviour under illumination. Methodologically I combine rapid electrodeposition, magnetron sputtering and CVD growth with electrochemical testing and spectroscopic/structural characterization. I have authored works in Advanced Energy Materials (first-author, 2023), Accounts of Materials Research (co-first, 2024), and delivered oral presentations at international conferences.

Projects

  • Programmable Electrodeposition for Rapid NH₄⁺-Storage Electrodes
    • First to regulate electrodeposition nucleation/growth to form nanocrystalline WO₃-based networks; a full electrode can be fabricated in ≈1000 s.
    • Architecture engineered for fast ion diffusion and efficient electronic transport (open porous paths, short diffusion length, continuous percolation).
    • Demonstrated high areal energy density up to 620 mWh·cm⁻² with robust cycling as an NH₄⁺-storage cathode.
    • Toolkit: potential/pulse programming, bath chemistry tuning, post-conversion to phase-pure WO₃.
    • Status: Submitted to Advanced Functional Materials (2025).
  • Photo-Assisted Ammonium-Ion Battery (PA-AIB)
    • Built photocathodes with WO₃/TiO₂ and WO₃/BDD heterojunctions to achieve favorable band alignment and efficient charge separation.
    • Under illumination, generated photovoltage lowers charge-transfer resistance and boosts capacity/rate for NH₄⁺ storage.
    • In/operando electrochemistry & spectroscopy used to quantify light-coupled kinetics and state evolution.
    • Device-level prototypes show stable light-enhanced output under practical intensities.
    • Status: In preparation.
  • Diamond Electrochemistry & Flexible “Diamond-Cloth” Devices
    • Explored interlayer-affected diamond electrochemistry; grew transferable/flexible diamond films via CVD and integrated with current collectors.
    • Developed diamond-cloth electrodes for durable supercapacitors; validated bending durability and long-term stability.
    • Contributions linked to works in Small Methods (2025) and J. Phys. Chem. C (2025).
