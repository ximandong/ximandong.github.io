About me

I am a joint Ph.D. candidate in Materials Engineering (University of Siegen) and Chemistry (Hasselt University). My research focuses on aqueous batteries and photoelectrochemical energy storage systems. Methodologically, I combine rapid electrodeposition, magnetron sputtering, and CVD growth with advanced electrochemical and spectroscopic analyses. Recently, I have also been exploring data-driven materials design, as well as battery thermal runaway and recycling strategies.

Projects

  • Nanocrystalline WO₃ for Rapid NH₄⁺-Storage Electrodes
    • Developed a folded nanocrystalline-stacked amorphous WO₃ electrode via rapid electrochemical deposition. The hierarchical architecture, provided abundant grain boundaries and oxygen vacancies for fast NH₄⁺ diffusion. The electrode delivered a high areal capacitance (2783 mF cm⁻²) and excellent cycling stability, achieving 620 mWh cm⁻² in a full ammonium-ion hybrid supercapacitor.
  • WO₃/TiO₂ Heterojunctions for Photo-Assisted Ammonium-Ion Battery (PA-AIB)
    • Constructed WO₃/TiO₂ composites with type-II band alignment to exploit photo–electrochemical synergy; island-like WO₃ nanocrystals on TiO₂ enhance charge separation and transport. Under illumination the electrode shows ~40% higher capacity and ~85% lower charge-transfer resistance, verifying efficient light-assisted NH₄⁺ storage.
  • WO₃/BDD p–n Junction Composite Electrode for Photo-Assisted Ammonium-Ion Battery
    • Designed a WO₃/BDD composite forming a p–n junction between n-type WO₃ and p-type boron-doped diamond. The built-in electric field enhanced photocarrier separation, while BDD ensured high conductivity and mechanical stability. The composite delivered ~1.7× higher photocurrent and ~20% higher capacitance under illumination, retaining >90% capacity after long-term cycling.
